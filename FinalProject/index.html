The Problem


Here are some challenges that have resulted from an increase in reliance on technology

Learn more about each challenge in the "Resources" tab



SOCIAL MEDIA


Social media can perpetuate certain ideas about how content should be displayed or made. On social media, algorithms play a big part in how artists present their work. From having a frequent posting schedule to following popular trends, an artist has to work around algorithms to ensure that their work is seen. Algorithms can prevent artists from reaching a larger audience due to their content not being labelled as "popular" or "relevant."



COMMERCE


Some people believe that due to the fact that it is online, digital art should be accessible to all. Many artists showcase their artworks freely online, while others have more exclusive works, where consumers have to pay to see them. The need to pay to see an artist's work, while being able to see another's freely, causes controversy. Many consumers believe that an artist should make their content free to the public because another one does.

The quality of virtual art can differ from that of physical art, leading to misconceptions of how one or the other should look. Digital art generally looks cleaner and more vibrant than photos of art on physical mediums, since physical art is more easily affected lighting. These factors create misconceptions that digital art is of higher quality than physical art.



ART THEFT


There are many instances where online art is stolen. Individuals and organizations alike steal artworks, edited or not, and claim them as their own. Many small artists are incapable of regaining rightful ownership of their art when a large organization steals it with little to no repercussions. Companies, such as Shein, are notorious for stealing from artists online.

Along with the rise in popularity of NFTs (non-fungible tokens), there has been a rise in the number of art thefts. Many profit off of digital art and, due to how accessible art on the Internet is, many will try to profit off of others' work.



ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE


As technology becomes more advanced, there has been a myriad of new ways to create art. Recently AI's are being used as a way to create art or even more abstract art, cutting out the need for human artists. As a result, artists struggle to get jobs, as their potential customers now have AIs that will get the job done at a much faster pace.
